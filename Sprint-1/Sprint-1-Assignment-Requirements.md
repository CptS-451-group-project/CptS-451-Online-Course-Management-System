# Objective

Plan and execute Sprint 1 for your project by identifying issues or user stories from the product backlog. Each team member must complete at least one feature by the end of the 21-day sprint. Use a Kanban board to track the progress and conduct a Sprint Review and Sprint Retrospective upon completion.

# Requirements

## 1. Sprint 1 Planning

### Product Backlog
Create or update your product backlog with all the features, issues, and user stories relevant to your project. Include:
- Unique ID for each item.
- Clear description of each feature/issue/user story.
- Priority level (High, Medium, Low).

### Sprint Planning Meeting
- As a team, select items from the product backlog to work on during Sprint 1.
- Each team member must pick at least one user story or issue to work on.
- Document the selected items in the Sprint Backlog with their estimated effort and acceptance criteria.

## 2. Sprint 1 Execution

### Kanban Board Setup
- Create a Kanban board with the following columns: To Do, In Progress, Code Review, Testing, Done.
- Place all Sprint 1 items in the To Do column initially.
- Move items across the board as they progress through each stage.

### Individual Contributions
- Each member should update the Kanban board daily to reflect their progress.
- Ensure all items assigned to you are moved to the Done column by the end of the Sprint.

## 3. Sprint 1 Review

### Sprint Review Meeting
- Present completed features/issues to the team.
- Demonstrate the functionality and ensure it meets the acceptance criteria.
- Discuss any items that were not completed and the reasons why.

### Documentation
- Prepare a brief summary of the work done, including completed items, any blockers encountered, and the outcome of the review meeting.

## 4. Sprint Retrospective

### Retrospective Meeting
- Discuss what went well during Sprint 1, what didn’t, and how the process can be improved.
- Document at least three action items for improvement based on the retrospective discussion.

### Retrospective Report
- Submit a one-page report summarizing the team's feedback and agreed-upon action items for future sprints.

# Sprint 1 Demo Video

Each team is required to submit a demo video showcasing their Sprint 1 activities. Use the template below to structure your video presentation.

The team lead will coordinate the video; however, every member must appear in the video and demonstrate their work.

## Demo Video Template

### Introduction (1-2 mins)
- Briefly introduce your team and project.
- State the main objectives of Sprint 1.

### Sprint Backlog Overview (2-3 mins)
- Show your Sprint Backlog and explain the selected features/issues.
- Describe each team member’s assigned task(s) and the expected outcomes.

### Kanban Board Walkthrough (2-3 mins)
- Demonstrate the state of the Kanban board at different stages of the Sprint.
- Highlight key movements and any blockers encountered.

### Database Design (4-5 mins)
- Identification of core entities and attributes
- Definition of primary keys and candidate keys
- Relationship mapping (1:1, 1:N, M:N)
- Clarification of cardinality and participation constraints
- Identification of weak entities (if any)
- Creation of initial ER diagram draft

### Feature Demonstration (5-7 mins)
- Present each completed feature in action.
- Explain the functionality, user stories, and how acceptance criteria were met.
- If any feature was not completed, briefly discuss the reasons.

### Sprint Review & Retrospective Summary (1-2 mins)
- Summarize the Sprint Review meeting outcomes, including completed and pending items.
- Share key points from the Sprint Retrospective and planned improvements for the next Sprint.

### Conclusion (1-2 mins)
- Reflect on the overall experience of Sprint 1.
- Provide a brief plan for the upcoming Sprint 2 activities.

## Video Guidelines
Ensure the video is clear, concise, and all team members participate in the presentation.

# Evaluation Criteria
- **Planning and Execution:** Effective use of the product backlog and Sprint planning to define clear goals for Sprint 1.
- **Kanban Usage:** Proper tracking and updating of items in the Kanban board throughout the Sprint.
- **Completion:** All team members must complete their assigned features/issues by the end of the Sprint.
- **Review and Retrospective:** Comprehensive review and honest retrospective analysis with actionable insights.
